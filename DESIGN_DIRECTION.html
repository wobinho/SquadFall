✓ STRENGTHS

  • Each gear is a complete, self-contained kit — 4 moves fill a full role
  • Simpler resource management, one pool per character
  • Card UI breathes — larger portrait, clearer hierarchy
  • Balance easier — gears are designed as complete units, not halves

— TRADEOFFS

  • Can feel shallow at high play counts once gears are memorized

DESIGN VERDICT: If you care about build expression, collectible hunting, combo play, and your UZI-style resource design, go 2-gear. The screen density is real but manageable with good visual hierarchy. If you care about clean identity, simpler balance, and more portrait space, go 1-gear. My honest lean for your vision: 2-gear. You mentioned the UZI idea unprompted — that kind of gear personality only shines when you can pair it with something that covers its weaknesses. In a 1-gear world, the UZI stands alone and has to do everything. In 2-gear, it's a sidearm that covers the shotgun's dry turns.
